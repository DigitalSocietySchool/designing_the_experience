WHAT IS CO-CREATION?

Co-creation is in general conceived as an initiative that brings different parties together, in order to jointly produce a mutually valued outcome. In the context of our product, Co-creation is considered as a methodology able to add new value to museum experiences by allowing potential visitors in collaboration with museum professionals to jointly co-construct more personalized and meaningful exhibition experiences.

WHY CO-CREATION?

Co-creation was selected amongst other methodologies for the opportunity it provides to its users to come up with relevant solutions to various problems through mutual understanding and respect.

WHAT ARE THE BENEFITS?

Co- creation can be beneficial by many respects. By applying Co- creation, museums are able to give voice and be responsive to the needs and interests of their local community members. Further, co-creative processes have a strong positive impact on the quality of results. These results take into account all the different perceptions, realities and interests of all the parties involved. Finally, Co- creation provides space for further community engagement and dialogue.

The Hive co-creation workshops are designed with the aim to invite the public to create and share their ideas, concepts, opinions and feelings surrounding a new museum exhibition. Through the process of a co-creation workshops community members can feel engaged with the museum in a different way and gain an important and contributory role on the design of upcoming exhibitions.

 

Museums on the other hand, can create a new relationship with their community, one which can lead to a new social standing and a new identity on the light of their future.

CO-CREATION WORKSHOPS

ACTIVITYCARDS

Activity cards constitute from a set of 20 cards specifically designed in accordance to research data showcasing some of the most important aspects that museum professionals take into account for the design of exhibitions. Alongside these cards, there were also 10 Media cards developed which represent interactive means that can be used within museum exhibitions. These cards are supplementary to the activity cards aimed to be used in order to add further interaction practices during concept creation. All 20 cards are available for workshop facilitators who define which one or more they will make use of for a co-creation workshop depending on their needs and the phase they are on exhibition design.

INSTRUCTION MANUAL

All the instructions regarding the efficient use of The Hive: Co-creation products and the best organization and facilitation of a co-creation workshop can be found on the instruction manual. This manual includes steps, suggestions, requirements and also explains clearly the framework that all Hive products are based on. It is really important to first read the general manual provided for all our products but also the smaller manuals provided for every single product.

GAME BOARD

One main element which differiates The Hive: Co-creation from other similar products on the market is the Game Board. Through a playful, colorful and most of all useful design we provide facilitators and participants a medium that they can use to structure and keep track of every step they take during a co-creation workshop. On every single board you will find its theme and relevant questions that help participants to better define and make sense of every single activity step in order to reach the best possible outcome. By the end of every co-creation workshop the Game Board is transformed into insights, concepts and ideas that museum professionals can analyze and ideally make use of.

DESINGING THE EXPERIENCE

The research group Experience Design for Crossmedia Content: CREAT-IT part of the Amsterdam University of Applied developed a new twenty weeks project (September 2015 to January 2016) entitled Designing the Experience in collaboration with MediaLAB Amsterdam and No More Mondays. Designing the Experience team constitutes of Angelo Croes, a Communication and Multimedia Design student at De Haagse Hogeschool, Konstantinos Dranganas a Mass Media Communication and Comparative Arts and Culture graduate and Angela Quintero an Industrial Designer graduate, under the supervision of the professional Applied Researcher Bernadette Schrandt. The Hive: Co-creation is designed with the aim to suggest and create new, diverging museum experiences by facilitating co-creation processes to involve all stakeholders in the design process.
